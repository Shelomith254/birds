Vultures are fascinating birds often misunderstood due to their appearance and feeding habits.
As nature’s clean-up crew, they play a vital role in the ecosystem by feeding on dead animals, preventing the spread of disease.
With incredibly strong stomach acid, they can safely digest rotting meat that would harm other animals.
Their bald heads help them stay clean while feeding, and their excellent eyesight allows them to spot carcasses from miles away.
Vultures are skilled gliders, able to soar for hours using thermal currents with minimal wing flapping.
Though they don’t sing like many birds, their silent nature is part of their uniqueness, using hisses and grunts to communicate.
In many cultures, vultures symbolize rebirth and cleansing, turning death into life by keeping the environment balanced and healthy.
